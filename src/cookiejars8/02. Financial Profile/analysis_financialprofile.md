FINAL FINDINGS/ANALYSIS

1. Does higher account balance increase likelihood of subscription?

Yes. Customers with higher balances tend to have higher subscription rates compared to lower-balance customers.

2. Does having a housing loan affect subscription probability?

Yes. Customers with housing loans generally show lower subscription rates than those without housing loans.

3. Does having a personal loan affect subscription behavior?

Yes. Customers with personal loans are typically less likely to subscribe.

4. Does having credit in default reduce subscription probability?

Yes. Customers in credit default have significantly lower subscription rates.

5. Is there an interaction between balance and loan status?

Yes. High-balance customers without loans are the most likely to subscribe, indicating that loan status can weaken the positive effect of higher balances.

6. Can subscription likelihood be linked to other products (e.g., loans)?

Yes. Customers with existing loans or credit obligations tend to subscribe at lower rates than those without additional financial products.

7. How many loans are personal vs housing loans?

Housing loans are more common than personal loans in the dataset.
